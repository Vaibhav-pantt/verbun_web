Discover Spanish History

Journey through the fascinating history of Spain — from Roman Hispania to the modern democracy.

Roman Hispania

For centuries, Hispania was part of the Roman Empire, bringing roads, law, and Latin to the Iberian Peninsula.

Al-Andalus

After the Muslim conquest in 711, much of Spain became a center of Islamic culture until the Christian Reconquista.

The Catholic Monarchs

Queen Isabella and King Ferdinand united Spain and completed the Reconquista in 1492, ushering in a global empire.

Spanish Empire

Under Charles I and Philip II, Spain became the first global empire, stretching across the Americas, Asia, and Europe.

Napoleonic Wars

Spain was invaded by Napoleon in 1808, sparking the Peninsular War and the rise of Spanish liberalism.

Franco Dictatorship

After the Spanish Civil War (1936–1939), Francisco Franco ruled Spain as a dictator until his death in 1975.

Modern Spain

Since the 1978 Constitution, Spain has developed into a parliamentary democracy and a key member of the EU.

Spanis Cinema

Volver

Directed by Pedro Almodóvar, this drama explores themes of family, memory, and resilience in modern Spain.

The Sea Inside

Alejandro Amenábar's moving biopic about Ramón Sampedro, a man fighting for his right to euthanasia.

Pan's Labyrinth

Guillermo del Toro’s fantasy drama set against the backdrop of post–Civil War Spain, blending magic and history.

Talk to Her

Another Almodóvar masterpiece, weaving the lives of two men and two women through emotional and ethical complexity.

Pedro Almodóvar

Iconic Spanish filmmaker known for colorful, emotional, and character-driven stories that explore identity and relationships.

Y Tu Mamá También

Alfonso Cuarón’s coming-of-age road movie that explores youth, friendship, and social divisions in Mexico.

The Orphanage

J.A. Bayona’s eerie thriller follows a woman revisiting her childhood orphanage with haunting consequences.

Julieta

Another Almodóvar drama, portraying a woman's emotional journey and the quiet sorrow of loss and estrangement.

Pain and Glory

A semi-autobiographical film by Almodóvar about a filmmaker reflecting on life, love, and creativity.

🎉 Spanish Culture & Traditions

Spanish culture is vibrant and diverse — known for flamenco music, colorful festivals, historic cities, and a deep appreciation for food, family, and the arts.

Art & Architecture

From Antoni Gaudí’s surreal Sagrada Família in Barcelona to the Moorish beauty of the Alhambra in Granada, Spanish architecture is a living museum of styles.

Spanish Cuisine

Spanish food is a social and flavorful experience — from tapas and paella to churros and jamón ibérico, every dish tells a story of the region it comes from.

Flamenco

Flamenco is the passionate art of southern Spain — a blend of music, song, and dance that expresses deep emotion and fiery spirit. It’s UNESCO-recognized cultural heritage.

Festivals & Fiestas

From Semana Santa processions to San Fermín’s running of the bulls, Spanish festivals are full of life, color, and community. Each region adds its own vibrant flavor.

La Tomatina

Held in Buñol, this iconic tomato-throwing festival is one of Spain’s wildest traditions, where thousands gather to joyfully pelt tomatoes in the streets.
